OpenAI’s GPT‑5: What we know so far

By The Blueprint Co. Team · June 8, 2026 · 5 min read

OpenAI has been tight‑lipped about its next flagship model, but a combination of official comments, leaks, and industry analysis paints a rough picture of what GPT‑5 will bring. The Blueprint Co. has gathered everything we know so far about the successor to GPT‑4.

Sam Altman confirmed in a recent Bloomberg interview that GPT‑5 is “in active development” and will be “materially better” than its predecessor. He did not give a release date, but most analysts expect a public launch by late 2026 or early 2027. The model is likely to be trained on a dataset several times larger than GPT‑4’s, possibly incorporating video and audio data for true multimodal understanding.

Leaked benchmarks suggest that GPT‑5 will score in the 90th percentile on the bar exam, up from GPT‑4’s 88th, and will demonstrate significant improvements in mathematics, coding, and reasoning. More importantly, OpenAI is expected to introduce a new architecture that allows the model to “think” longer on complex problems, similar to the chain‑of‑thought reasoning already seen in competitors like Anthropic’s Claude 3.5.

One open question is how GPT‑5 will be priced. GPT‑4 currently costs $20 per month for ChatGPT Plus. With the massive compute required for the new model, there is speculation that OpenAI may introduce a new premium tier or raise prices. The company recently closed a $40 billion funding round, which should cover training costs, but profitability remains a long‑term concern.

Regulators are also watching closely. The EU AI Act’s transparency requirements for general‑purpose AI models will apply to GPT‑5, meaning OpenAI must disclose details about its training data and implement safeguards against misuse. How the company balances openness with competitive secrecy will be a key challenge.

The Blueprint Co. will continue to follow GPT‑5 developments and report on their implications for users and the AI industry.

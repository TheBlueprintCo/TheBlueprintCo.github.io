All AI‑generated content must now be watermarked under new global treaty

By The Blueprint Co. Team · June 12, 2026 · 6 min read

A landmark international agreement signed today requires all synthetic media – from text to video – to carry a cryptographically verifiable watermark. The Global AI Transparency Treaty, backed by 45 nations including the US, EU member states, Japan, and South Korea, aims to combat deepfakes and disinformation by making AI‑generated content instantly identifiable.

The treaty mandates that any content created by generative AI systems must embed a watermark that survives simple edits like cropping, compression, or re‑encoding. Major AI developers including OpenAI, Google, Meta, and Stability AI have agreed to implement the standard, known as C2PA 2.0, within 90 days. Non‑compliant platforms could face fines and market‑access restrictions.

“This is the most significant step yet in restoring trust to digital media,” said Dr. Rumman Chowdhury, an AI ethics researcher advising the treaty negotiations. “But the real test will be whether these watermarks can withstand sophisticated removal attempts – and what we do when they don't.”

Privacy advocates have raised concerns that mandatory watermarking could chill anonymous expression. The treaty includes an exception for “small‑scale personal use” – meaning an individual's private messages or personal art projects are exempt. However, any content published on platforms with more than 100,000 users must be marked.

Implementing the treaty will be a significant challenge. It requires all devices that generate AI content – including smartphones running on‑device models – to include watermarking hardware. The treaty's technical annex suggests this could be achieved through software updates, but older devices may be left behind.

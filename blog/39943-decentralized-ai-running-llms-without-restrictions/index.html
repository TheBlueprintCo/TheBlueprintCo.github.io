Decentralized AI: Running LLMs Without Restrictions

By The Blueprint Co. Team · June 5, 2026 · 7 min read

If you’re tired of hitting AI content filters, the ultimate solution is to take the model offline. Decentralized, locally‑hosted large language models have become surprisingly accessible in 2026, thanks to efficient open‑source releases and lightweight runtimes.

We tested the top three local setups: LLaMA 3.1 (70B), Mixtral 8x22B, and Command R+. All can run on a single high‑end consumer GPU or a rented cloud server. With a custom system prompt, they have zero censorship.

Step‑by‑step: We recommend starting with Ollama or LM Studio – both are free and require no command‑line expertise. Download the model, load it, and you have a private, unrestricted assistant.

One caveat: local models still require careful prompt engineering to avoid generating harmful content unintentionally. But the control is entirely in your hands, and there is no third party watching your queries.

For those who need mobile access, we detail how to set up a private API on a home server with Tailscale, so you can securely access your uncensored model from anywhere.

The Blueprint Co. Team believes that decentralization is the most reliable long‑term answer to AI censorship.
